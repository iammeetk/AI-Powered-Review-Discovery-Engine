# Spotify Discovery Review Analysis

## Data Sources Used

- App Store: 78 retained records
- Play Store: 46 retained records
- Reddit: 42 retained records
- Spotify Community: 17 retained records

## Key Findings

1. Search and browse friction is the largest friction area, appearing in 134 of 183 retained records.
2. The dominant user intent is to find a specific song or artist, indicating the product job is active exploration rather than passive playback.
3. Active explorers are the clearest affected segment, with needs that differ from casual listeners.
4. Sentiment is mostly unresolved: 74% of feedback is negative or mixed.
5. Users often describe a control gap: they can skip or hide content, but do not trust that Spotify understands the reason.
6. Repeated listening is usually caused by history overpowering momentary intent, not by a lack of interest in discovery.

## User Segments

- General Spotify listeners: 83 signals (45%)
- Active explorers: 45 signals (25%)
- Comfort-loop listeners: 39 signals (21%)
- Mood and context curators: 14 signals (8%)
- Niche and long-tail fans: 12 signals (7%)
- Social discovery users: 11 signals (6%)
- Control seekers: 8 signals (4%)

## Opportunities

- **Familiar-to-fresh control**: A visible control would let users choose comfort listening or exploration without training the whole account incorrectly. Signals: Repetitive recommendations, Low novelty in discovery surfaces, Library history overpowers exploration.
- **Recommendation feedback with reason codes**: Users need hide/dislike/reset actions that communicate whether the issue is artist, song, mood, popularity, or repetition. Signals: Insufficient recommendation controls, Weak context or mood understanding.
- **Exploration mode for niche and adjacent artists**: A mode that dampens mainstream popularity and library history can serve users seeking smaller releases or adjacent genres. Signals: Niche and long-tail fans, Active explorers.
- **Context-aware discovery surfaces**: Separate work, gym, night, social, and nostalgia contexts so discovery does not collapse into a single profile. Signals: Weak context or mood understanding, Collaborative discovery gaps.
